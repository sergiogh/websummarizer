		<img thumbnail="" width="1920" height="1080" src="https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform.png" class="attachment- size- wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async" srcset="https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform.png 1920w, https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform-300x169.png 300w, https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform-1024x576.png 1024w, https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform-768x432.png 768w, https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform-1536x864.png 1536w, https://qcentroid.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/qcentroid-makes-nvidia-cuda-q-available-in-its-quantumops-platform-416x234.png 416w" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px" />
<p><em>The integration brings GPU-accelerated quantum workflow development, benchmarking, and experiment lifecycle management into a unified enterprise QuantumOps framework.</em></p>



<p>BILBAO, SPAIN&nbsp;— 16 March 2026 — QCentroid, the enterprise QuantumOps platform for orchestrating and integrating quantum and hybrid optimization workflows, today announced that <a href="https://developer.nvidia.com/cuda-q"><strong>NVIDIA CUDA-Q</strong></a><strong> is now integrated and available within the QCentroid platform</strong>. The capability enables enterprise teams to develop and evaluate hybrid quantum-classical workflows using NVIDIA accelerated computing alongside quantum backends, with a structured approach to experimentation, benchmarking, and lifecycle management.</p>



<p>QCentroid has been part of the <a href="https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups/"><strong>NVIDIA Inception</strong></a><strong> program since 2024</strong>, and this release extends QCentroid’s mission: helping organizations navigate the “pre-quantum advantage” era through disciplined hybrid experimentation, strong baselines, and reproducible results,&nbsp; while remaining ready to scale adoption as quantum hardware evolves.</p>



<p>“Enterprise adoption doesn’t happen through one-off demos, it happens through repeatable workflows, governance, and credible benchmarking,” said <strong>Carlos Kuchkovsky, CEO of QCentroid</strong>. “By making CUDA-Q available inside QCentroid QuantumOps, teams can combine GPU-based development and baselining with quantum execution paths in a single operational framework, reducing friction from discovery to experimentation and comparison”.</p>



<p>“Breakthroughs towards quantum-GPU supercomputers are being made when users work in a truly hybrid environment,” said Sam Stanwyck, Director of Quantum Product at NVIDIA. “Through its integration with the CUDA-Q platform, Qcentroid now gives users the GPU acceleration they need to explore tomorrow’s quantum applications today.”<em></em></p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Enabling hybrid execution and benchmarking with CUDA-Q inside QuantumOps</strong></p>



<p>By using NVIDIA CUDA-Q within QuantumOps, enterprise teams can leverage GPU-accelerated simulations of quantum computing, streamline execution pipelines and reduce friction when running quantum and hybrid workflows as part of broader experimentation and benchmarking cycles.</p>



<p>This enables organizations to shorten time-to-learning and accelerate iteration loops, one of the most persistent bottlenecks in real-world quantum adoption, by providing a consistent operational layer for:</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<div class="wp-block-advgb-list"><ul class="advgblist-54403f0a-6182-4b15-b719-9d0b06645029">
<li>Hybrid workflow orchestration</li>



<li>Benchmark design and execution cycles</li>



<li>Comparative analysis across approaches and configurations</li>



<li>Traceability and reporting for enterprise decision-making<a id="_msocom_1"></a></li>
</ul></div>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Agentic vision for quantum adoption: AI-assisted workflow acceleration</strong></p>



<p>To help enterprises enter the benchmarking loop faster, QCentroid complements CUDA-Q execution enablement with an AI Copilot layer (Quantum expert agents) that converts early exploration into an execution-ready use-case pack: a full use-case definition, initial model formulation, required data schema, recommended solver and backend paths, and starter solver code, to speed up the most resource-intensive steps of the adoption lifecycle.</p>



<p>By combining CUDA-Q support, orchestration, benchmarking, and agentic workflows, QuantumOps enables organizations to build a structured, evidence-based adoption pathway, from exploration to measurable progress.</p>



<p><strong>Learn more: </strong>Tech article<strong>: </strong><a href="/blog/how-to-accelerate-enterprise-quantum-computing-adoption-using-nvidia-and-cuda-q-with-a-quantum-ops-framework-approach/">How to accelerate enterprise Quantum computing adoption using Nvidia and CUDA-Q with a Quantum ops framework approach</a>.</p>

<p></p>



<p><strong>About QCentroid</strong></p>



<p>QCentroid is an enterprise QuantumOps platform for orchestrating, benchmarking, and integrating hybrid quantum-classical optimization workflows across CPUs, GPUs, and multiple quantum backends. Headquartered in Bilbao, Spain, QCentroid provides a structured experimentation loop, workflow orchestration, benchmark design, experiment tracking, traceability, and reporting, so teams can make evidence-based decisions in the pre-quantum-advantage era and scale adoption as hardware evolves. QCentroid also offers an AI Copilot layer that accelerates use cases from definition to execution-ready development.</p>
