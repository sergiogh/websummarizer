    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Every aircraft carries a flight recorder. Your cryptographic infrastructure almost certainly does not.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>That's the uncomfortable parallel at the heart of PQC migration. We've built enterprise cryptographic estates over decades layered protocols, vendor libraries, hardware modules, certification dependencies and we're now attempting a large-scale transformation of that estate with, in most cases, no structured record of what's running, how it's configured, or what would break first.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Cryptographic BOMs (CBOMs) are gaining traction as the answer. But there's a critical distinction that gets lost in most of the conversation: a CBOM designed for inventory compliance and a CBOM designed for migration are not the same artifact.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>An inventory CBOM answers: </span><span class="italic">"What cryptography is present, and does it meet today's policy?"</span><span class> </span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>A migration CBOM answers: </span><span class="italic">"What must change, who must coordinate, and what is blocking activation of quantum-safe modes?"</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Here's what makes the migration-oriented profile different and why it matters:</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Configuration observability beyond asset lists. It's not enough to know RSA-2048 is present. A migration CBOM records where it's applied, how it's configured at runtime, which interfaces and trust boundaries depend on it, and whether a quantum-safe mode exists in that deployment. Without this, you can't reason about blast radius or readiness.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>The black box for your migration programme. When something breaks during a transition, or an audit asks why a system wasn't migrated on schedule, the migration CBOM is your record of what was known, what was planned, and what dependencies were blocking progress. It's accountability infrastructure, not just a compliance artefact.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Dependency chains, not just local assets. Most enterprises aren't blocked by their own code  they're blocked by vendors, protocols, and partners who haven't yet shipped quantum-safe capabilities. A migration CBOM surfaces the full ecosystem dependency chain: which external parties are load-bearing, what their roadmaps look like, and when activation actually becomes possible.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>A living roadmap, not a point-in-time snapshot. Vendor PQC availability dates, hybrid deployment windows, hardware refresh cycles, and certification re-scoping timelines all belong in the migration CBOM. This turns it from a compliance PDF into an executable planning artefact you can version, query, and align to procurement and regulatory deadlines.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>The coordination substrate for multi-org migration. Large-scale quantum-safe transition requires procurement, security architecture, vendors, and regulators to work from the same data. A standardized migration CBOM profile with consistent field semantics and profile identifiers — is the shared language that makes cross-organizational readiness assessment possible rather than a manual, inconsistent exercise.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>The prerequisite for automation. Readiness scoring, gap analysis, and policy enforcement all require structured, machine-readable migration data. Without a defined migration CBOM profile, these capabilities remain aspirational. With it, automated tooling can validate compliance, surface blockers, and track progress across business units continuously.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>The flight recorder analogy holds. We don't fly aircraft and hope nothing goes wrong. We instrument them so that when something does, we know what happened — and we use that data proactively to improve safety. PQC migration is a multi-year, multi-stakeholder programme operating in a complex dependency environment. The migration CBOM is how we instrument it.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
  
<!----><!---->                  

    <div class="article-main__content" data-test-id="publishing-text-block">
        <p>
          <span class>Interested in how IBM Research is thinking about CBOM profiles for quantum-safe transition including what a </span><span class="italic">CBOM.pqcmigration</span><span class> profile actually needs to capture? Happy to share more in the comments.</span><!---->
        </p>
    </div>
