February 2, 2026 —</strong> QuEra Computing and Roadrunner Venture Studios today announced a $4M strategic partnership to build a quantum testbed at the Roadrunner Quantum Lab (RQL) powered by the State of New Mexico in Albuquerque. QuEra’s commitment includes running facilities with full-time hires in state, making Albuquerque’s Innovation District a world-class center for quantum companies.</p><p>“QuEra is one of the most innovative quantum companies in the world and they are now bringing their neutral atom platform to New Mexico,&quot; said Adam Hammer, CEO and Co-Founder of Roadrunner Venture Studios. “Together, QuEra, Roadrunner, and the State of New Mexico are removing a significant burden that New Mexico-based founders face in validating their technologies: access to leading engineers, top-of-market equipment, and compute. This new facility will be an unparalleled proving ground for America&#x27;s next generation of quantum companies.”</p><p>The partnership is a significant economic investment in New Mexico and the first step in a long-term commitment to the state. QuEra will establish a physical presence at the RQL and hire multiple full-time employees, while expanding access to its quantum computing systems for resident companies. The testbed is designed to generate high-skill jobs, workforce training programs, startup formation, and increased federal and private capital—building the foundation for a durable advanced technology economy in New Mexico.</p><p>&quot;To build a quantum economy, companies need to test and prove their technologies quickly, efficiently, and accurately,&quot; said Nate Gemelke, Chief Technology Strategist of QuEra Computing. “This partnership brings infrastructure to New Mexico that fills a real gap in getting quantum technology to market. With the help of our New Mexico partners, QuEra’s vision is to bring more resources to New Mexico in the coming years and make the state a national hub for neutral-atom quantum computing.”</p><p>The collaboration is the latest accomplishment in the state’s $300 million effort to build a globally competitive quantum economy. By testing on QuEra’s systems, founders and scientists can speed up expensive, time-consuming builds and deliver and deploy technology more efficiently.</p><p>The testbeds will support R&amp;D across multiple domains, including:</p><ul><li><strong>Photonics and Optics Testing Center. </strong>The POTC will be a laboratory with equipment for Neutral Atom Quantum Computing research. This infrastructure will provide approved users with remote access to QuEra-hosted compute and quantum resources. This infrastructure will enable vetted researchers, academic partners, and industry collaborators to remotely run, develop, and test algorithms, and perform complex simulations from anywhere.</li></ul><p>The facilities are expected to launch later this year, starting with academic and national lab partners before opening to industry collaborators and quantum startups.
