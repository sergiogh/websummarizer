The carbon nanotube gatemon qubit | Nature Communications

In a 2025 Nature Communications article, H. Riechert and colleagues report the first coherent control of a gatemon qubit based on a single carbon nanotube integrated into a circuit quantum electrodynamics (cQED) system. Utilizing a hexagonal boron nitride substrate, this architecture achieved record coherence times for carbon nanotube-based qubits—200 ns, surpassing previous nanotube qubit implementations (typically ~10 ns). The device leverages the one-dimensionality and minimal electronic degrees of freedom of carbon nanotubes, with charge noise identified as the key decoherence factor. This work not only demonstrates the feasibility of superconducting carbon nanotube qubits but also paves the way for probing microscopic fermionic processes in low-dimensional quantum conductors, marking a significant step forward for hybrid quantum architectures in 2025.

Universal quantum computation using Ising anyons from a non-semisimple topological quantum field theory | Nature Communications

In a paper published August 5, 2025, in Nature Communications, Filippo Iulianelli, Sung Kim, Joshua Sussan, and Aaron D. Lauda introduce a novel topological quantum computation (TQC) framework rooted in non-semisimple topological quantum field theory (TQFT). Traditional Ising anyons, found in the ν = 5/2 fractional quantum Hall (FQH) state, are limited to Clifford gates by braiding, thus falling short of universal quantum computation. The authors demonstrate that by extending the Ising model with a single new anyon type—emerging from a non-semisimple TQFT—universal quantum computation via braiding alone becomes possible. This approach, leveraging robust topological invariants and new anyonic properties, reinvigorates the prospect for scalable, fault-tolerant quantum computing platforms in 2025.
