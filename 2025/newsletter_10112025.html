On-chip quantum interference of indistinguishable single photons from integrated independent molecules | Nature Nanotechnology

Researchers led by Tailin Huang et al. published in *Nature Nanotechnology* (Nov 2025) report the successful on-chip Hong–Ou–Mandel quantum interference using indistinguishable single photons emitted from independent molecules. Utilizing a molecular quantum photonic chip composed of a single-crystalline organic nanosheet, they integrated more than 100 waveguide-coupled molecules—tuned to the same frequency via Stark tuning—without nanofabrication, thus minimizing spectral diffusion. Achieved interference visibilities exceeded 0.97 for two individually coupled molecules, with over 100-µs quantum beating observed when a controlled frequency difference was introduced. This approach provides a scalable path for optical quantum processors and advances the study of waveguide quantum electrodynamics using identical integrated single emitters—a notable step toward scalable optical quantum technologies in 2025.

[2511.05465] Helios: A 98-qubit trapped-ion quantum computer

Quantinuum’s team, led by Anthony Ransford and colleagues, reports on “Helios,” a 98-qubit trapped-ion quantum computer utilizing the quantum charge-coupled device (QCCD) architecture and $^{137}$Ba$^{+}$ hyperfine qubits. Helios incorporates all-to-all connectivity enabled by a rotatable ion storage ring, parallelized operations for speed, and real-time software compilation. Across its operational zones, Helios achieves average single-qubit gate infidelities of $2.5(1)\times10^{-5}$, two-qubit gate infidelities of $7.9(2)\times10^{-4}$, and state preparation and measurement infidelities of $4.8(6)\times10^{-4}$. The reported performance is not fundamentally limited and can likely be improved. Demonstrated operation in random circuit sampling exceeds classical simulation capabilities, advancing the current fidelity and complexity frontier for quantum computers in 2025.
