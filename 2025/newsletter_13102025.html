What happened: Kipu Quantum demonstrated Hybrid Sequential Quantum Computing—runtime‑adaptive sequencing of classical search, digitized counterdiabatic quantum evolution, and classical refinement—on higher‑order unconstrained binary optimization using IBM’s 156‑qubit heavy‑hex processors. Why it matters: Orchestrated stages delivered runtime quantum‑advantage level and avoided higher‑order‑to‑quadratic mapping and embedding overhead seen on annealers. Key numbers/people: Reached the ground state in ~3 s vs ~22 s for memetic tabu search and ~1,800 s for simulated annealing (up to 700× and 9× faster); matched or exceeded exact solver CPLEX (~23 s) while faster. Direct impact: Faster near‑optimal or exact solutions on current digital processors, advancing real‑world optimization across logistics, manufacturing, and telecom.
