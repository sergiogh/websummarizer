Industrial applications and high‑performance computing - Aramco’s Upstream Digital Center and NVIDIA demonstrated Dammam‑7Q, one of the region’s largest quantum computing emulators, on the Dammam‑7 Supercomputer using NVIDIA CUDA‑Q to develop subsurface imaging algorithms, including quantum Hadamard edge detection. The work emulated up to 30 quantum bits per graphical processing unit, scaled across multiple graphical processing units, detected three‑dimensional seismic faults on a full three‑dimensional dataset, and marked an Aramco–NVIDIA collaboration to explore hybrid quantum‑classical supercomputers (Ashraf M. Al‑Tahini; Tim Costa). Education and workforce development - Qilimanjaro Quantum Tech and QURECA Ltd signed a global collaboration (Barcelona/Glasgow, October 14, 2025) to advance quantum education, training, and talent development, led by Marta P. Estarellas and Araceli Venegas‑Gomez. Qilimanjaro will offer hands‑on access via its SpeQtrum hybrid platform to analog and digital hardware built with fluxonium analog qubits, while QURECA provides international training, professional certification, and skills development through joint initiatives, workshops, and content. Implication - Across multiple European announcements above, stakeholders explicitly expanded hardware access (Basque Quantum Initiative System Two, D‑Wave European deployment), funding (LuxQuanta), and policy support (Joint Research Centre).

Blog by Clayton Leite: QMill sets a world record in quantum circuit compression

What happened: QMill unveiled an artificial intelligence–driven, world‑record method for quantum circuit compression, outperforming the state‑of‑the‑art optimizer Quarl under equivalent computational budgets, and released benchmark and compressed circuits. Why it matters: Shorter circuits reduce gate counts and runtime, addressing noise and decoherence that derail deep computations. Key numbers/people: MOD5_4 cut from 71 to 24 gates (versus Quarl’s 50); GF2^8_MULT from 928 to 740 (versus 821); CSUM_MUX_9 from 459 to 290 (versus 318). Direct impact: QMill states this compression brings useful quantum computations closer in time and helps circumvent obstacles posed by today’s noisy hardware.
