Terra Quantum Advances Quantum Error Correction With Novel Technique

Terra Quantum has introduced QMM-Enhanced Error Correction, a hardware-validated quantum error correction technique inspired by quantum gravity, aiming to address scalability in quantum computing. The approach uses a Quantum Memory Matrix (QMM)—a finite-dimensional lattice analogous to space-time—to encode and protect quantum information, focusing on the management of 'unit errors.' Unlike traditional methods requiring heavy physical qubit overhead, this method leverages QMM’s geometric structure to detect and correct errors efficiently within the hardware. While performance metrics and peer-reviewed validation are pending, the technique demonstrated a significant reduction in errors during controlled tests. This research, presented at a quantum information conference and funded by a private foundation, suggests a new pathway for building error-resilient and scalable quantum computers in 2025.

Haiqu and Xanadu work with the Open Quantum Design initiative for an open-source quantum computer

Haiqu, Xanadu, and Open Quantum Design, backed by $1.6 million from Canada's Digital Technology Supercluster (within a $4.1 million CODE project budget), are collaborating to deliver the world's first full-stack open-source quantum compilation infrastructure. The approach aims to remove vendor lock-in, reduce development costs, and foster faster, more democratic access to quantum technology at a time when under 10,000 quantum professionals worldwide limit progress.

[2508.06251] Synthetic Data Generation and Differential Privacy using Tensor Networks' Matrix Product States (MPS)

Researchers Alejandro Moreno R. et al. present a method for generating high-quality, privacy-preserving synthetic tabular data using Matrix Product States (MPS)—a class of tensor networks—with integrated differential privacy techniques such as noise injection and gradient clipping, leveraging Rényi Differential Privacy accounting. Benchmarked against CTGAN, VAE, and PrivBayes, the MPS-based model demonstrated superior fidelity and privacy performance, especially under stringent privacy constraints. The study emphasizes MPS as an interpretable and scalable solution for secure synthetic data generation, offering promise for sensitive domains requiring both data quality and confidentiality. In 2025, this work positions tensor network approaches as relevant tools at the intersection of quantum-inspired methods, machine learning, and practical privacy requirements.

Quantum computing meets AI to fight cancer

At the ACS Fall 2025 meeting, Christoph Gorgulla of St. Jude Children's Research Hospital presented quantum computing research merging AI to accelerate drug discovery against the cancer-related protein KRas. Leveraging a training set of 1.1 million KRas-targeting molecules, the quantum model generated thousands of candidate drugs, which AI then refined. After iterative filtering, the team synthesized and tested 15 molecules, identifying two promising inhibitors, ISM061-018-2 and ISM061-022. Both showed efficacy and safety in cell-based assays. Although “it’s an encouraging early step,” according to Angela Wilson (Michigan State University), Gorgulla acknowledged the work has not achieved quantum advantage, noting further increases in quantum processing power are likely needed to outperform classical computing within four to five years.

Quantum-safe security: Progress towards next-generation cryptography

Microsoft is accelerating preparations for quantum-safe security, recognizing quantum computing’s future threat to current public-key cryptography. Collaborating with organizations like NIST, IETF, ISO, OCP, and ETSI, Microsoft aims to establish quantum-safe encryption standards and global interoperability. Efforts include contributions to four NIST PQC algorithm submissions, experimental deployment of PQC-protected VPN tunnels (e.g., Project Natick), and co-development of the FrodoKEM cryptosystem, set to become an ISO standard. In 2024, Microsoft released the open-source Adams Bridge Accelerator for quantum-resilient cryptography and integrated it with Caliptra2.0 via OCP. Preview rollouts of PQC features for Windows Insiders and Linux, as well as SymCrypt updates, highlight commitment to crypto-agility and proactive migration to quantum-safe architectures amid industry transition.
