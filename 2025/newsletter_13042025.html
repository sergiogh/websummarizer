THE SCALABILITY AND ERROR CORRECTION IMPERATIVE Even with the progress in 2025, fault tolerance remains the holy grail of quantum computing. The mention of 72-qubit systems (Wukong), IonQ’s 28-qubit tests, or even IBM’s 433-qubit Osprey chip does not necessarily imply large-scale fault tolerance. Error rates remain in the 10^-3 to 10^-4 range for gate operations in some of the best superconducting and ion-trap systems. Surface codes or topological codes demand overhead expansions that can multiply the physical qubit count by factors of 100–1,000 to produce a single logical qubit with extremely low error. DARPA’s QBI is partially designed to see which companies can break through that overhead barrier or significantly reduce it. Some focus on hardware-level optimizations (e.g., better transmon designs, fluxonium qubits, photonic fusion-based approaches, or spin-based qubits in isotopically purified silicon). Others emphasize advanced error-correcting codes that can handle correlated noise or hardware crosstalk. As 2025 closes, the race intensifies: the winners might be those with tangible error-corrected quantum subcircuits that can be scaled in modular fashion. XXI.
