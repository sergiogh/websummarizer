[2505.06532] Micius, the world's first quantum communication satellite, was hackable

In a remarkable discovery, Alexander Miller has revealed that Micius, the world's first satellite for quantum communications, is hackable. The decoy-state BB84 protocol employed for quantum key distribution (QKD) on Micius is considered secure against photon-number-splitting eavesdropping; however, Miller's analysis shows otherwise. A detailed examination of the ground station-to-satellite communication data brought to light relative time delays of up to 300 ps between the eight onboard lasers. This delay, in turn, compromised the unconditional security of QKD, providing an opportunity for potential attackers to distinguish between decoy and signal states in 98.7% case. This finding highlights potential pitfalls in the experimental implementation of QKD, demanding rigorous security considerations for quantum communication systems.
