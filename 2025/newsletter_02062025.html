GitHub - qec-codes/QuantumThreatTracker: A tool for quantifying the threat quantum computers pose to public-key cryptosystems.

Open-source repository, GitHub has introduced 'QuantumThreatTracker', a tool to quantify the risk quantum computers impose on public-key cryptosystems. It essentially uses data to make estimates on when quantum computers will be strong enough to break public-key cryptographic protocols like Rivest-Shamir-Adleman (RSA) and Diffie-Hellman (DH) that heavily rely on the computational hardness of mathematical problems. As quantum computers improve, the capacity for them to jeopardize various cryptographic protocols increases, creating a fluctuating threat landscape. This novel utility, currently in its proof of concept, was funded by Ofgem and jointly developed by Cambridge Consultants and the University of Edinburgh. The evolving quantum computing landscape underscores the urgency for tools like QuantumThreatTracker.
