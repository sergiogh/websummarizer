NSF National Quantum Virtual Laboratory speeds into the design phase

The U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF) announced a $16 million investment in four teams to design the National Quantum Virtual Laboratory (NQVL), a shared resource to democratize access to quantum hardware and software across the U.S. Each team—comprising universities, federal agencies (including DOE, DOD, NIST, NASA), and over 20 companies such as IonQ, J.P. Morgan, NVIDIA, and QuEra—will receive $4 million over two years. Projects include networked shareable quantum computers and a "digital twin" for testing algorithms. Brian Stone (performing NSF director duties) calls it "a critical bridge between basic discovery and deployment," aiming to convert America’s quantum science leadership into practical technologies. NSF plans to select more teams and expand funding as appropriations allow. This marks a major federally coordinated push toward accessible quantum R&D infrastructure in 2025.
