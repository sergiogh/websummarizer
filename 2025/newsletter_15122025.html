[2512.05322] Highly resilient, error-protected quantum gates in a solid-state quantum network node

E. Poem, M. I. Cohen, S. Blum, D. Minin, D. Korn, O. Heifler, S. Maayani, A. Hamo, I. Bayn, N. Bar-Gill, and M. Tordjman report the first experimental realization of error-protected conditional gates in solid-state systems using nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers in diamond. Their new theoretical framework, Power-Unaffected, Doubly-Detuning-Insensitive Gates (PUDDINGs), enables robust quantum gates immune to both amplitude and frequency errors. Room-temperature randomized benchmarking showed error per gate reduced by up to a factor of 9. Projecting to cryogenic temperatures, the team estimates a two-qubit error per gate of $1.2 \times 10^{-5}$ (99.9988% fidelity)—exceeding thresholds for surface and color code error correction, marking a significant advance for fault-tolerant solid-state quantum networks in 2025.
