Title:A log-depth in-place quantum Fourier transform that rarely needs ancillas

Researchers Gregory D. Kahanamoku-Meyer, John Blue, Thiago Bergamaschi, Craig Gidney, and Isaac L. Chuang, proposed a new quantum circuit model that adopts an 'optimistic' approach. Their work, submitted to ArXiv in May 2025, potentially allows a log-depth in-place quantum Fourier transform (QFT) that does not frequently need ancillary qubits. They develop an "optimistic quantum circuits" concept, addressing the often cheaper approximation on most inputs over all inputs in quantum circuit design. The in-place QFT circuit implies that it has tunable error parameter, uses no ancillas, is measurement-free, and has a depth at the cost of O(log(n/ ε)). This development threads a promising path to factoring circuits of nearly linear depth and creates potential for achieving an asymptotically optimal depth with fewer ancilla qubits. Future advancements in quantum computing may lean on this 'optimism'.
