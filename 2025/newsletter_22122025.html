Magic state cultivation on a superconducting quantum processor

A team led by Emma Rosenfeld and 294 co-authors reports a landmark experimental study of magic state cultivation on a superconducting quantum processor (arXiv:2512.13908, submitted 15 Dec 2025). They demonstrate that cultivation reduces error by a factor of 40, reaching a state fidelity of 0.9999(1), while retaining 8% of attempts. The experiment includes code-switching into a surface code and a fault-tolerant protocol to bound magic state fidelity. This work experimentally establishes magic state cultivation as a viable approach to providing high-fidelity non-Clifford gates, which have been a resource bottleneck in fault-tolerant quantum computing, signaling a crucial step toward practical quantum error correction architectures in 2025.

An 11-qubit atom processor in silicon

A team led by Hermann Edlbauer and Michelle Y. Simmons published in Nature (2025) the demonstration of an 11-qubit atom processor in isotopically purified silicon-28 using precision-placed phosphorus atoms. This platform, called 14|15, features two multi-nuclear spin registers connected by a fast, exchange-based link. The team achieved high-fidelity single- and multi-qubit gates (99.10%–99.99%) and measured Bell-state fidelities up to 99.5%. Entanglement was demonstrated for up to eight nuclear spins, and all combinations of local and non-local qubit pairs were tested. This marks a tripling of coupled qubit counts for atom-based silicon devices while maintaining gate fidelities above 99%, representing a foundational step toward expanding error-corrected quantum computing with scalable silicon technology.

Silicon Quantum Computing | SQC Establishes Leadership in Silicon Modality of Quantum Computing with Record-Setting Processor

Silicon Quantum Computing (SQC), based in Sydney, has achieved a major breakthrough in silicon-based quantum processors. Published in Nature on December 18, 2025, SQC’s multi-qubit, multi-register processor demonstrates fidelities up to 99.99%, with qubit quality improving as the system scales—a reversal of the usual trend toward lower quality at higher qubit counts. SQC manufactures its own quantum chips with atom-level (0.13 nm) precision, enabling superior benchmarking (e.g., Grover’s algorithm). Recent milestones include progressing to Stage B of DARPA’s Quantum Benchmarking Initiative, Telstra’s reported efficiency gains in model training, and an Australian Defence datacenter deployment.
