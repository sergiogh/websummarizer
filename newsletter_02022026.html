Scaling Sample-Based Quantum Diagonalization on GPU-Accelerated Systems using OpenMP Offload

Researchers led by Robert Walkup and collaborators have demonstrated significant acceleration of sample-based quantum diagonalization—a hybrid quantum and high-performance computing method—by using graphics processing units as the main compute engines. The study adapted quantum chemistry algorithms for graphics processing unit architectures via OpenMP offloading, targeting the most resource-intensive step: classical diagonalization of Hamiltonian matrices. Benchmarks on six graphics processing unit-accelerated systems, including the Frontier supercomputer, report performance boosts of around one hundred times per node, reducing processing from hours to minutes. This advancement brings classical computation time in line with quantum execution durations, facilitating more efficient study of complex chemical systems and expanding the practical potential of hybrid quantum-classical workflows in material and molecular research.
