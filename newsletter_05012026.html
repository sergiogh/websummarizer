SOFT: a high-performance simulator for universal fault-tolerant quantum circuits

Researchers Riling Li, Keli Zheng, Yiming Zhang, Huazhe Lou, Shenggang Ying, Ke Liu, and Xiaoming Sun have introduced SOFT, a high-performance simulator for universal fault-tolerant quantum circuits. By combining generalized stabilizer formalism with optimized graphics processing unit parallelization, SOFT can simulate noisy, non-Clifford circuits, previously impractical at scale. Demonstrating this, the team performed over 200 billion simulations of the latest magic state cultivation protocol at code distance five, managing 42 qubits and 72 T or T-dagger gates with mid-circuit measurements, using only modest graphics processing unit resources. The simulation provided the first ground-truth validation at this scale and identified a significant discrepancy between observed and reported logical error rates, underscoring the value of robust simulation tools in developing fault-tolerant quantum architectures.

Laura Lewis, Dar Gilboa, and Jarrod R.
